# Social Media Content Mastery

## Overview
Create engaging social media content that builds audience, drives engagement, and generates leads. This skill helps solo entrepreneurs grow on LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and other platforms without a social media team.

---

## When to Use This Skill

- Building personal brand on social media
- Creating daily/weekly social content
- Growing LinkedIn or Twitter following
- Driving engagement and conversations
- Generating inbound leads from social
- Repurposing long-form content

## Core Framework: Hook-Story-Offer

Every high-performing social post follows this structure:

### **Hook** (First 1-2 lines)
**Goal:** Stop the scroll

**Techniques:**
- Bold statement ("Most founders fail at cold outreach.")
- Surprising stat ("80% of sales happen after the 5th follow-up.")
- Relatable problem ("Your LinkedIn posts get 3 likes. All from your mom.")
- Question ("What if I told you cold email still works in 2025?")
- Contrarian take ("Stop posting daily on LinkedIn.")

**Examples:**
```
‚úÖ "I closed $2M in deals without a single cold call."
‚úÖ "Your cold emails aren't working. Here's why:"
‚úÖ "Most sales advice is terrible. Do this instead:"
‚úÖ "I analyzed 1,000 LinkedIn posts. Here's what actually works:"
```

---

### **Story** (Middle 5-10 lines)
**Goal:** Build connection + provide value

**Structure:**
- Context (the situation)
- Conflict (the challenge)
- Resolution (what you learned/did)
- Insight (the takeaway)

**OR tactical value:**
- Framework breakdown
- Step-by-step process
- Mistakes to avoid
- Tools/resources

**Example (Story-based):**
```
Last year, I sent 500 cold emails.
Got 2 replies. Both said "not interested."

I was ready to give up on outbound entirely.

Then I changed one thing: personalization.

Instead of "I noticed your company..." I wrote:
"Saw you posted about [specific thing]. Here's what worked for us..."

Reply rate jumped from 0.4% to 12%.
```

**Example (Tactical):**
```
Here's the framework I use:

S - Subject (3-6 words, create curiosity)
P - Personalize (reference specific trigger)
A - Agitate (state the pain)
R - Result (share proof)
K - Keep it simple (one clear CTA)

Used this to close $500K+ in pipeline.
```

---

### **Offer** (Last 1-3 lines)
**Goal:** Drive action

**Types of CTAs:**
- **Engagement:** "What's worked for you?" (drives comments)
- **Resource:** "DM me 'template' for the full guide" (builds list)
- **Lead gen:** "Free cold email templates in my bio" (drives traffic)
- **Soft sell:** "Want help with this? Let's chat" (starts conversations)
- **Social proof:** "Helped 50+ founders do this. You're next."

**Examples:**
```

‚úÖ "Want the full framework? Link in comments."
‚úÖ "DM me 'SPARK' and I'll send you the template."
‚úÖ "Repost this if it helped ‚ôªÔ∏è"
```

## Platform-Specific Strategies

### LinkedIn

**Post formats that work:**
1. **Personal stories** (lessons learned, failures, wins)
2. **Tactical frameworks** (step-by-step processes)
3. **Contrarian takes** (challenge conventional wisdom)
4. **Listicles** ("5 ways to...", "3 mistakes...")
5. **Case studies** (specific results with numbers)

**Optimal post length:**
- **Short-form:** 100-200 words (quick insights)
- **Medium-form:** 200-400 words (storytelling)
- **Long-form:** 400-800 words (deep dives)

**Best practices:**
- **Post 3-5 times per week** (consistency > volume)
- **First 2 lines are CRITICAL** (hook or they scroll)
- **Line breaks:** 1-2 sentences per line (mobile-friendly)
- **Emojis sparingly:** One per post max (professional)
- **Tag strategically:** Only relevant people (don't spam)
- **Engage in first hour:** Reply to every comment

**Post structure template:**
```
[Hook - bold statement or question]

[Blank line]

[Context - set up the story]
[Conflict - the challenge]
[Resolution - what happened]

[Blank line]

[Insight - the lesson]

[Blank line]

[Tactical takeaway - specific tip]

[Blank line]

[CTA - question to drive comments]
```

### Twitter/X

**Post formats that work:**
1. **Single tweets** (quick insights, hot takes)
2. **Threads** (step-by-step guides, stories)
3. **Quote tweets** (add commentary to others' content)
4. **Polls** (engagement, audience research)

**Character limits:**
- **Single tweet:** 280 characters
- **Threads:** Break into 5-15 tweets

**Best practices:**
- **Post 5-10 times per day** (high volume platform)
- **Mix formats:** 60% value, 30% engagement, 10% personal
- **Use threads** for longer content
- **Reply to others:** Spend 50% time engaging
- **Hashtags:** 1-2 max (not as important as LinkedIn)

## Content Calendar Planning

### Weekly Posting Schedule

**LinkedIn (3-5 posts/week):**
- **Monday:** Motivational / lesson learned
- **Wednesday:** Tactical framework / how-to
- **Friday:** Case study / results

**Twitter (5-10 posts/day):**
- **Morning:** Insight / quick tip
- **Midday:** Engage with others (replies, quote tweets)
- **Afternoon:** Thread (once per week)
- **Evening:** Personal / behind-the-scenes

---

### Content Batching Process

**Time-block 2-3 hours per week:**

**Monday (1 hour): Planning**
- Review analytics (what performed well?)
- Brainstorm 10-15 topic ideas
- Check calendar for relevant events/launches

**Tuesday (2 hours): Creation**
- Write 3-5 LinkedIn posts
- Write 10-15 Twitter posts
- Create 1 thread

**Wednesday (30 min): Scheduling**
- Schedule all posts for the week
- Tools: Buffer, Hootsuite, Typefully (Twitter)

**Daily (30 min): Engagement**
- Reply to comments on your posts
- Engage with others' content
- DM conversations

## Analytics & Optimization

### Metrics to Track

**LinkedIn:**
- **Impressions:** How many saw your post
- **Engagement rate:** (Likes + comments + shares) / impressions
- **Profile views:** Are posts driving discovery?
- **Followers:** Growth rate

**Twitter:**
- **Impressions**
- **Engagement rate:** (Likes + RTs + replies) / impressions
- **Link clicks:** If sharing resources
- **Follower growth**

**Targets:**
- **Engagement rate:** 2-5% (LinkedIn), 1-3% (Twitter)
- **Follower growth:** 5-10% per month
- **Profile views:** 500-1,000/week

---

### Content Testing Framework

**Test one variable at a time:**

**Hook types:**
- Bold statement vs. question vs. stat
- Which gets more engagement?

**Post length:**
- Short (100 words) vs. long (400 words)
- Does length matter?

**CTA types:**
- Engagement ask vs. resource offer
- Which drives more comments/DMs?

**Posting time:**
- Morning (8-10am) vs. midday (12-2pm) vs. evening (4-6pm)
- When does your audience engage most?

**Track results for 4 weeks** then double down on what works.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå No clear hook
**Problem:** People scroll past
**Fix:** First 1-2 lines must grab attention

### ‚ùå Wall of text
**Problem:** Unreadable on mobile
**Fix:** 1-2 sentences per line, add white space

### ‚ùå Too salesy
**Problem:** Looks like spam
**Fix:** 90% value, 10% promotion

### ‚ùå No engagement
**Problem:** One-way broadcast
**Fix:** Reply to every comment within 1 hour

### ‚ùå Inconsistent posting
**Problem:** Algorithm buries you
**Fix:** Post 3-5x/week minimum

### ‚ùå Ignoring analytics
**Problem:** Don't know what works
**Fix:** Review weekly, double down on winners

## Pro Tips

### Golden Hour Engagement
First 1 hour after posting is critical:
- Reply to EVERY comment
- Like every comment
- Ask follow-up questions
- Algorithm rewards early engagement

### Borrow Authority
Quote or tag industry leaders:
```
"As @[expert] said: '[quote]'

Here's what I've learned applying this..."
```
They might engage, exposing you to their audience.

### The 1-3-30 Rule
For every 1 post you make:
- Comment on 3 others' posts
- Spend 30 minutes engaging

Build relationships, not just followers.

### Save High-Performers
Create a swipe file of your best posts:
- What hook worked?
- What structure?
- What CTA?
Reuse winning formulas.

### Use Scheduling Tools
**LinkedIn:** Buffer, Hootsuite
**Twitter:** Typefully, Tweet Hunter, Buffer

Batch create, schedule, engage daily.
