# Value Propositions & Business Cases

## Overview
Craft executive-ready value narratives, ROI stories, and business cases for prospects. This skill helps you translate discovery insights into quantified impact stories that win deals and secure budget approval.

---

## When to Use This Skill

- Creating proposals for qualified opportunities
- Building business cases for budget approval
- Preparing executive presentations
- Responding to RFPs with value narratives
- Justifying premium pricing
- Competing against cheaper alternatives

---

## Core Framework: Value Story Structure

Every compelling value proposition follows this hierarchy:

### 1. **Headline Impact**
State the quantified outcome upfront

**Format:**
```
[Prospect] will [achieve X outcome] in [timeframe] by [implementing solution]
```

**Examples:**
- "Acme Corp will save $500K annually in sales operations costs within 6 months"
- "Your team will increase pipeline velocity 40% and close 3 weeks faster within 90 days"
- "Eliminate 15 hours of manual work per rep per week starting Month 1"

**Why this works:**
- Grabs attention immediately
- Makes the outcome tangible
- Sets expectations for the story

---

### 2. **Driver Pillars**
Break impact into 3-4 specific drivers

**Structure:**
```
The $500K savings comes from:
1. [Driver 1]: $200K from reduced manual data entry
2. [Driver 2]: $180K from improved win rates
3. [Driver 3]: $120K from faster ramp time
```

**Requirements:**
- Must add up to headline impact
- Include supporting data or benchmarks
- Connect to discovery insights (their specific pain)

---

### 3. **Proof Layer**
Attach evidence to each driver

**Types of proof:**
- **Customer stories:** "[Similar Company] achieved [metric] in [timeframe]"
- **Product metrics:** "Our avg customer sees [outcome] within [timeline]"
- **Analyst stats:** "Gartner reports [industry benchmark]"
- **Internal data:** "Your current [metric] vs. benchmark"

**Example:**
```
Driver 1: $200K from reduced manual data entry
Proof: "SalesLoft eliminated 12 hours/week of data entry per rep using our automation. With 20 reps @ $75/hr, that's $936K annual savings for them."
```

---

### 4. **Execution Plan**
Outline how you'll deliver the value

**Include:**
- Implementation phases (30/60/90 days)
- Required resources (from their side + yours)
- Success metrics per phase
- Risk mitigation

**Template:**
```
Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Setup & onboarding
- Actions: [List]
- Resources: [List]
- Success metric: [Metric]

Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Rollout to pilot team
- Actions: [List]
- Success metric: [Metric]

Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Full deployment
- Success metric: [Full impact achieved]
```

---

### 5. **Decision Ask**
Specify what you need from them

**Format:**
```
To move forward, we need:
1. [Budget approval by date]
2. [Technical requirements met]
3. [Executive sponsor assigned]
4. [Timeline commitment]

Next step: [Specific action with date]
```

## Assumptions Documentation

**CRITICAL:** Always document assumptions in ROI calculations

### Template:
```markdown
## Assumptions

This ROI model is based on the following assumptions gathered during discovery:

1. **Team size:** [X reps]
2. **Avg hourly rate:** $[Y] (based on [source])
3. **Current time spent on [task]:** [Z hours/week] (per rep)
4. **Adoption rate:** [%] (based on [similar customer benchmark])
5. **Ramp time:** [Months to full adoption]
6. **Benchmark data:** [Source: Similar customer / Industry report]

### Sensitivity Analysis
| Assumption | Conservative | Base Case | Optimistic |
|------------|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Time saved | [X hrs] | [Y hrs] | [Z hrs] |
| ROI | [Low %] | [Base %] | [High %] |
| Payback | [Long] | [Base] | [Short] |
```

**Why this matters:**
- Builds trust (transparent calculations)
- Allows prospect to adjust inputs
- Addresses "seems too good to be true" concerns
- CFO/finance teams demand this level of rigor

## Pro Tips

### Mirror Their Language
- Use their exact words for problems/outcomes from discovery
- Reference their internal metrics and KPIs
- Match their formality level (startup vs. enterprise)

### Anchor to Their Goals
- Tie value to company OKRs or annual initiatives
- Connect to executive's personal success metrics
- Reference board/investor expectations

### Make Assumptions Transparent
- Show your math
- Offer sensitivity ranges (conservative/base/optimistic)
- Invite them to adjust inputs

### Pair with Proof
- Every driver needs a customer story or benchmark
- Use logos/names when possible (with permission)
- Include before/after screenshots or metrics

### Keep Executive Version to 1 Page
- Detailed appendix for deep-dive
- One-pager for decision makers
- They don't have time for 20-page decks
